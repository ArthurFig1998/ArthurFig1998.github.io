"How can I build Zion in my own apartment?"

Ponder: In preparation for this meeting, read and answer the following:

  • The prophet Brigham Young declared, "I have Zion in my view constantly. We are not going to wait for angels, or for Enoch and his company to come and build up Zion, but we are going to build it" ("Building Up and Adornment of Zion By the Saints", Journal of Discourses, Volume 9(56), pp. 282-285).

  • How can you help your students see the benefit of building Zion in their apartments?

Discuss: Select one of the three options below to lead students in a group discussion. This discussion will help students identify how they can build Zion in their own apartments. As you prepare, consider additional questions that will enhance the discussion. Remember to allow students time to think and participate.

Read Moses 7:18-19 and discuss the following questions and quote:

  • After reading the verses, what should Zion look like in my apartment?
  • As an apartment, what do you need to do today to build Zion in your apartment?
  • "Zion is Zion because of the character, attributes, and faithfulness of her citizens. . . . We cannot wait until Zion comes for these things to happen - Zion will come only as they happen" (Elder D. Todd Christofferson, "Come To Zion").
  • Some roommates may not have an interest in having Zion in their apartment or prioritizing roommate relationships. What can a student do to build Zion in this situation?

Moses 7:18-19

18 And the Lord called his people Zion, because they were of one heart and one mind, and dwelt in righteousness; and there was no poor among them.

19 And Enoch continued his preaching in righteousness unto the people of God. And it came to pass in his days, that he built a city that was called the City of Holiness, even Zion.

Watch Light: A Student Living Apartment and discuss the following questions:

  • What principles did you observe the students practicing in this apartment that contribute to Zion?
  • How can an apartment be righteously unified and guided by the Holy Ghost despite various perspectives?
  • If your roommates don't understand the values of an apartment filled with light, how can you help them?
  • When conflict and/or contention occurs, what can you do to restore the light in the apartment?

Watch this video about the principles of Zion and discuss the following questions:

  • Why is it important to take ownership of building Zion?
  • What can roommates struggling to get along do to build Zion in their apartment?
  • Share ways you and your roommates can implement the principles Zion together in your apartment.

Activity:

The following activity is intended to help students work together using the principles they have discussed to see how they can “build Zion”.

  • Break into small groups and provide each group with the same building materials of your choice (some examples are: basic wooden blocks, marshmallows & toothpicks, etc.). Have them compete in a timed race to see which group can build the tallest structure without it collapsing.

  • At the conclusion of the activity, have students share what they have learned.

Invite & Follow Up:

Invite everyone to have an apartment council to discuss with their roommates how they want to establish Zion in their own apartments.


Follow up in person, email, or text about what they established.
